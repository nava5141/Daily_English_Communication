Meeting Summary – “Who is Your Role Model?” (31-03-2026)
1. Narender Mekala
Key Points
His mother is his main role model.
Highlighted both:
Positive role model (mother)
Negative role model (father – behavior he doesn’t want to repeat)
Described his mother’s:
Lifelong sacrifice and selflessness
Focus only on family (no personal enjoyment)
Hardships in village life (mud house, poverty)
Achievements due to her sacrifice:
Family now owns a house worth ₹25–30 lakhs
Emotional connection:
Calls his mother a “hero”
Often becomes emotional while speaking about her
Life lesson:
“Life success depends on how you end, not how you start”
Conclusion (Narender)
A true role model is someone who sacrifices selflessly and uplifts family despite hardships.
2. Navakumar K
Key Points
Agreed that mothers are the backbone of middle-class families.
Observed behaviors of his mother:
Takes responsibility without expecting help
Always solves others’ problems
Acts as first responder in crises
Personal experience:
After graduation stress, his mother motivated him to keep trying instead of blaming
Highlighted her:
Supportive nature
Problem-solving mindset
Avoidance of blame
Conclusion (Navakumar)
A role model is someone who supports, guides, and teaches problem-solving without negativity.

Key Points

Most participants identified family members (especially mothers and grandparents) as their role models.
A true role model is someone who:
Sacrifices selflessly
Supports during difficult times
Guides without blaming
Works hard to improve future generations
The discussion highlighted that:

Emotional values and real-life struggles create stronger role models than external figures
